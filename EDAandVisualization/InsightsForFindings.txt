Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) – Key Findings and Insights
1. Dataset Overview
The dataset consists of 524,202 records and 19 features, providing a large and diverse collection of used car auction data.
The large number of records ensures that the analysis is statistically meaningful and suitable for identifying market trends.
2. Data Quality
The dataset is completely cleaned, with no missing values present in any column.
Duplicate records were checked and removed during the data cleaning phase, ensuring the reliability and consistency of the analysis.
3. Statistical Summary
Numerical variables such as selling price, MMR, odometer reading, condition, and car age exhibit a wide range of values.
The presence of high maximum values compared to the average suggests that the dataset includes both economy vehicles and premium or luxury vehicles.
4. Correlation Analysis
The correlation heatmap indicates that MMR (Manheim Market Report) has the strongest positive correlation with selling price, making it one of the most influential factors in determining vehicle value.
Car age and odometer reading show a negative relationship with selling price, indicating that older and heavily driven vehicles generally have lower resale values.
The engineered feature price ratio also demonstrates a positive relationship with selling price, confirming its usefulness in the analysis.
5. Selling Price Distribution
The selling price distribution is positively skewed (right-skewed), meaning that most vehicles are sold in the low-to-medium price range.
Only a small percentage of vehicles belong to the high-price luxury segment, resulting in a long right tail in the distribution.
6. Top Car Manufacturers
The dataset is dominated by popular automobile manufacturers such as Ford, Chevrolet, Toyota, Nissan, and Honda.
Luxury manufacturers appear less frequently, indicating that the used car auction market primarily consists of mass-market vehicles.
7. Average Selling Price by Body Type
Vehicle body style has a noticeable impact on resale value.
SUVs, pickup trucks, and luxury vehicle categories generally have higher average selling prices compared to sedans and hatchbacks.
This suggests that vehicle size, utility, and market demand influence pricing.
8. Car Age vs. Selling Price
A clear negative relationship exists between car age and selling price.
As vehicles become older, their resale value generally decreases due to depreciation.
Newer vehicles consistently achieve higher selling prices in the auction market.
9. Odometer vs. Selling Price
The scatter plot shows that vehicles with higher odometer readings generally sell at lower prices.
Low-mileage vehicles command higher resale values because they are perceived to have less wear and tear.
Although mileage significantly affects price, other factors such as brand, condition, and model also contribute to price variation.
10. Overall Conclusion
The exploratory data analysis demonstrates that vehicle resale prices are influenced by multiple factors, including market valuation (MMR), vehicle age, mileage, manufacturer, and body type.
Among these factors, MMR is the strongest predictor of selling price, while depreciation and mileage have a negative impact on resale value.
The dataset is well-structured, free from missing values, and suitable for further predictive modeling and machine learning applications.
